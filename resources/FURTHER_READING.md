## Core Articles

### The 20% Problem — Why Coding Speed Isn't the Point

- **Bain & Company** (2025). ["From Pilots to Payoff: Generative AI in Software Development."](https://www.bain.com/insights/from-pilots-to-payoff-generative-ai-in-software-development-technology-report-2025/)
  Coding/testing is 25–35% of idea-to-launch time. AI coding gains of 10–15% often don't translate into business outcomes.

- **Nikhil Sachdeva** (December 2025). ["The AI-Native Engineering Flow."](https://medium.com/data-science-at-microsoft/the-ai-native-engineering-flow-5de5ffd7d877)
  Microsoft experiment: one engineer + six AI agents. Implementation dropped to single digits; 73% became strategic work. Agent personas at [github.com/niksacdev/engineering-team-agents](https://github.com/niksacdev/engineering-team-agents).

### Agentic SDLC and Role Evolution

- **Ioan Salau** (January 2026). ["Is Agile Dead? The Rise of the Agentic SDLC and What It Means for Your Career."](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/agile-dead-rise-agentic-sdlc-what-means-your-career-ioan-salau-1hfbf/)
  Frames how AI changes the entire SDLC, not just coding. Argues for role transformation over role creation.

- **Mark Russinovich & Scott Hanselman** (February 2026). "Redefining the Software Engineering Profession for AI." *Communications of the ACM.* [DOI: 10.1145/3779312](https://doi.org/10.1145/3779312)
  AI is seniority-biased: seniors get a multiplier, juniors face "AI drag." Proposes preceptorship at scale to protect the talent pipeline.

### Industry Signals

- **Aki Ito / Business Insider** (2026). "The Block Layoffs Are Just the Beginning."
  Block cut ~50% of its workforce. Jack Dorsey: "intelligence tools" let a smaller team run the company. An ML engineer who used AI daily was still laid off.

- **Matt Shumer** (February 2026). "Something Big Is Happening."
  Argues the current moment is a "Feb 2020 moment." Models are helping build models. Acceleration is non-linear.

- **Citrini Research / Alap Shah** (2026). "The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis."
  Thought experiment: what happens when agentic automation scales into consumption, finance, and services. "Ghost GDP" and the intelligence premium unwind.

### Where Agents Are Deployed

- **Ted Gulesserian** (2026). [LinkedIn post on Anthropic domain data.](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/ted-gulesserian-5086a6_anthropic-just-showed-where-agents-are-actually-activity-7431378439003222016-oIcV)
  ~50% of agent tool usage is in software engineering. Adoption is early and concentrated.

## Concepts to Explore Further

### Agentic Engineering
- The difference between using AI passively (accepting suggestions) and using AI agentically (framing, directing, verifying, deciding)
- Socratic AI interaction: hypothesis → constraints → validation plan before code generation

### The Full SDLC with AI
- Requirements: AI-drafted PRDs from meeting transcripts
- Architecture: Retroactive ADRs from code and PR discussions
- Testing: AI-generated test cases that catch spec-vs-code gaps
- Security: Pre-screening diffs before human review
- Code review: AI first pass, human judgment second pass
- Reporting: Automated status synthesis from project data

### Career Development
- Why foundational engineering skills (algorithms, architecture, systems thinking) matter *more* with AI, not less
- The preceptorship model: how to grow from junior to senior in an AI-augmented world
- "Hold strong opinions weakly" — your tools will change; your judgment must grow
